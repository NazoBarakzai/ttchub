TTC Hub Story

The Technical Training Center Hub began as a small initiative with a big vision: to bridge the gap between traditional education and industry-ready skills. Founded by a group of educators and industry experts, the center started with just a few workshops in trades like welding, electrical work, and automotive repair. Word quickly spread as students experienced hands-on learning, gaining not only technical expertise but also confidence to pursue meaningful careers. Over the years, the hub expanded its programs to include modern fields like CNC machining, HVACR, web design, graphic design, culinary arts, and supply chain logistics. Today, the hub stands as a beacon for practical education, where innovation meets tradition. Its mission remains clear: empower individuals with the skills, certifications, and real-world experience they need to thrive in technical industries, while fostering a community where learning is active, collaborative, and transformative. see more . .

Donor

Government of Balochistan

Government of Balochistan serves as the primary donor and supporter of the Technical Training Center Hub, playing a vital role in its establishment, funding, and development. Through its commitment to skill development and youth empowerment, the government provides financial resources, infrastructure, and policy support to ensure the effective functioning of the center. It also works closely with institutions like the Technical Education and Vocational Training Authority to design relevant training programs, monitor quality standards, and align courses with market needs. In addition, the government facilitates partnerships with national and international organizations to enhance training capacity, upgrade equipment, and improve instructional methods. Special focus is given to inclusivity by introducing programs for women, marginalized groups, and unemployed youth, ensuring equal access to skill development opportunities. By acting as a donor and enabler, the Government of Balochistan aims to create a skilled workforce capable of contributing to industries and exploring employment opportunities beyond the province. Ultimately, this support reflects the government’s vision of reducing poverty, boosting economic growth, and promoting self-reliance among people.

Adopted by

SZABIST ZABTech (iTVE)

SZABIST ZABTech is a leading vocational and technical education network in Pakistan operating under Shaheed Zulfikar Ali Bhutto Institute of Science and Technology. It provides demand-driven technical and practical training to youth, particularly in underdeveloped and semi-urban regions. One of its major initiatives is the ZABTech Technical Training Center Hub, established to improve access to quality technical education and support the industrial sector of Balochistan. The center offers short courses and diploma programs in electrical work, mechanical maintenance, welding, civil construction, information technology, and other industry-related trades. These programs are designed with industry consultation to provide practical and job-ready skills. The center aims to empower local youth with employable skills that meet industrial demands. Graduates can secure jobs locally and internationally, contributing to economic development. ZABTech also promotes inclusivity by providing opportunities for women and disadvantaged communities, along with career counseling, internships, and job placement support. The institute also organizes workshops, seminars, and industrial visits to enhance students’ practical exposure. Modern training methods and updated equipment help students gain hands-on experience in their respective fields. Through these efforts, ZABTech continues to strengthen technical education and develop a skilled, professional, and competitive workforce for the future. In conclusion, the ZABTech Technical Training Center in Hub plays an important role in skill development, employment generation, and industrial growth through quality technical education.

Monitoring Agency

B-TEVTA

Balochistan Technical Education and Vocational Training Authority (BTEVTA) is the main government organization responsible for promoting technical and vocational education in Balochistan. Operating under the Government of Balochistan, it develops skilled manpower by establishing and supporting Technical Training Centers (TTCs) across the province, including remote and underdeveloped areas. Its goal is to provide skill-based education and create better employment opportunities for the youth of Balochistan and surrounding industrial regions. The Technical Training Centers adopted by BTEVTA offer practical and industry-focused training in trades such as electrical work, plumbing, welding, refrigeration and air conditioning, information technology, and automotive mechanics. These programs focus on hands-on learning so students gain practical experience along with theoretical knowledge and become ready for modern industries and job markets. BTEVTA also works with industries and organizations to improve training quality through updated curricula, instructor training, and modern equipment. In addition, the authority encourages participation from women and disadvantaged communities by promoting inclusive educational opportunities. Through its efforts in skill development and workforce training, BTEVTA continues to play an important role in reducing unemployment and supporting the economic growth of Balochistan.

Implementing Partner

BPPPA

The collaboration between TTC and its implementing partner, the Balochistan Public Private Partnership Authority (BPPPA), represents a strategic effort to strengthen development initiatives in the region through effective public-private collaboration. By leveraging the expertise, resources, and institutional capacity of both entities, this partnership aims to deliver sustainable infrastructure and service solutions tailored to the needs of Balochistan. As an implementing partner, BPPPA plays a crucial role in facilitating project design, ensuring regulatory compliance, and promoting transparency in execution. Its mandate to foster partnerships between government bodies and private sector stakeholders aligns closely with TTC’s development objectives, creating a shared framework for innovation and efficiency. Through this collaboration, TTC benefits from BPPPA’s local knowledge and governance structures, enabling smoother project implementation and stronger community engagement. At the same time, BPPPA gains from TTC’s technical expertise and strategic approach, enhancing its ability to deliver impactful public-private partnership projects. Overall, the TTC–BPPPA partnership stands as a model for cooperative development, demonstrating how aligned goals and shared responsibilities can lead to meaningful and lasting outcomes in Balochistan.
