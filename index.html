TTC Hub Story

The Technical Training Center Hub began as a small initiative with a big vision: to bridge the gap between traditional education and industry-ready skills. Founded by a group of educators and industry experts, the center started with just a few workshops in trades like welding, electrical work, and automotive repair. Word quickly spread as students experienced hands-on learning, gaining not only technical expertise but also confidence to pursue meaningful careers. Over the years, the hub expanded its programs to include modern fields like CNC machining, HVACR, web design, graphic design, culinary arts, and supply chain logistics. Today, the hub stands as a beacon for practical education, where innovation meets tradition. Its mission remains clear: empower individuals with the skills, certifications, and real-world experience they need to thrive in technical industries, while fostering a community where learning is active, collaborative, and transformative. see more . .

Partners

Donor

Government of Balochistan

The Government of Balochistan serves as the primary donor and supporter of the Technical Training Center Hub, playing a vital role in its establishment, funding, and overall development. Through its commitment to skill development and youth empowerment, the government provides financial resources, infrastructure, and policy support to ensure the effective functioning of the center. It also works closely with institutions like the Technical Education and Vocational Training Authority to design relevant training programs, monitor quality standards, and align courses with market needs. In addition, the government facilitates partnerships with national and international organizations to enhance training capacity, upgrade equipment, and improve instructional methods. Special focus is given to inclusivity by introducing programs for women, marginalized groups, and unemployed youth, ensuring equal access to skill development opportunities. By acting as a donor and enabler, the Government of Balochistan aims to create a skilled workforce capable of contributing to local industries and exploring employment opportunities beyond the province. Ultimately, this support reflects the government’s long-term vision of reducing poverty, boosting economic growth, and promoting self-reliance among its citizens.

Adopted by

SZABIST ZABTech (iTVE)

SZABIST ZABTech is a well-known vocational and technical education network in Pakistan, working under the umbrella of the Shaheed Zulfikar Ali Bhutto Institute of Science and Technology. It focuses on providing demand-driven skills and practical training to youth, especially in underdeveloped and semi-urban regions. One of its important initiatives is the establishment and operation of the ZABTech Technical Training Center in Hub, a key industrial city in Balochistan. This center was adopted to enhance access to quality technical education for the local population and to support the growing industrial sector in the area. The ZABTech Hub training center offers a variety of short courses and diploma programs in trades such as electrical work, mechanical maintenance, welding, civil construction, information technology, and other industry-relevant skills. The training programs are designed in consultation with industry experts to ensure that students gain practical, job-ready competencies. A major objective of this center is to empower the youth of Hub by providing them with employable skills. Since Hub is an industrial zone with factories and manufacturing units, the availability of skilled labor benefits both job seekers and employers. Graduates from the center can find employment locally or even abroad, contributing to economic development. In addition, ZABTech emphasizes inclusivity by offering opportunities for women and disadvantaged communities. The institute often provides career counseling, internships, and job placement support to help trainees transition smoothly into the workforce. In conclusion, the ZABTech Technical Training Center in Hub represents a significant effort by SZABIST ZABTech to promote skill development, reduce unemployment, and support industrial growth in Balochistan through quality technical education.

Monitoring Agency

B-TEVTA

Balochistan Technical Education and Vocational Training Authority (BTEVTA) is the main government body responsible for promoting technical and vocational education in the province of Balochistan. It operates under the Government of Balochistan to develop skilled manpower by establishing and adopting Technical Training Centers (TTCs) across the region. BTEVTA has adopted a wide network of Technical Training Centers to provide structured, practical, and industry-focused training to young people. These centers are located in different districts, including remote and underdeveloped areas, to ensure equal access to skill development opportunities. The Technical Training Centers offer various courses in trades such as electrical work, plumbing, welding, refrigeration and air conditioning, information technology, and automotive mechanics. The training programs focus on hands-on learning so that students gain practical experience along with basic theoretical knowledge. One of the main goals of BTEVTA in adopting these centers is to reduce unemployment and improve living standards in the province. By aligning training programs with market needs, the authority ensures that students develop skills that are in demand in both local and international job markets. BTEVTA also works with different organizations to improve the quality of training by updating course content, training instructors, and providing modern equipment. In addition, special programs are introduced to encourage participation from women and support disadvantaged communities. In conclusion, BTEVTA’s adoption of Technical Training Centers shows its strong commitment to developing skills, creating job opportunities, and supporting the economic growth of Balochistan.

Implementing Partner

BPPPA

The collaboration between TTC and its implementing partner, the Balochistan Public Private Partnership Authority (BPPPA), represents a strategic effort to strengthen development initiatives in the region through effective public-private collaboration. By leveraging the expertise, resources, and institutional capacity of both entities, this partnership aims to deliver sustainable infrastructure and service solutions tailored to the needs of Balochistan. As an implementing partner, BPPPA plays a crucial role in facilitating project design, ensuring regulatory compliance, and promoting transparency in execution. Its mandate to foster partnerships between government bodies and private sector stakeholders aligns closely with TTC’s development objectives, creating a shared framework for innovation and efficiency. Through this collaboration, TTC benefits from BPPPA’s local knowledge and governance structures, enabling smoother project implementation and stronger community engagement. At the same time, BPPPA gains from TTC’s technical expertise and strategic approach, enhancing its ability to deliver impactful public-private partnership projects. Overall, the TTC–BPPPA partnership stands as a model for cooperative development, demonstrating how aligned goals and shared responsibilities can lead to meaningful and lasting outcomes in Balochistan.
